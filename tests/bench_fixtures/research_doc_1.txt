Title: Retrieval-Augmented Generation for Knowledge-Intensive NLP Tasks
Authors: Lewis et al., 2020

Abstract: Large pre-trained language models store factual knowledge in their parameters
but struggle with knowledge-intensive tasks. We propose RAG — models that combine
pre-trained parametric memory with a non-parametric retrieval component. RAG models
retrieve relevant passages from a dense vector index and condition generation on them.
On open-domain QA, RAG sets new state-of-the-art on three benchmarks, outperforming
extractive and closed-book approaches. The retriever is trained end-to-end alongside
the generator, enabling it to learn what to retrieve for downstream tasks.

Key findings:
- RAG-Sequence and RAG-Token marginalize over retrieved documents differently
- End-to-end training of retriever + generator outperforms pipeline approaches
- RAG generates more specific, diverse, and factual text than pure parametric models

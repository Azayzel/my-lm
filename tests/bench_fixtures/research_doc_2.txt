Title: Dense Passage Retrieval for Open-Domain Question Answering
Authors: Karpukhin et al., 2020

Abstract: Open-domain question answering relies on efficient passage retrieval to find
relevant contexts. We show that a simple dual-encoder framework trained on QA pairs —
Dense Passage Retrieval (DPR) — significantly outperforms traditional sparse methods
like BM25. DPR uses BERT-based encoders for questions and passages, trained with
in-batch negatives and hard negatives mined from BM25. On multiple QA benchmarks, DPR
improves top-20 passage retrieval accuracy by 9–19% over BM25 and leads to substantial
gains when plugged into extractive QA pipelines.

Key findings:
- Dense representations outperform TF-IDF/BM25 for passage retrieval in QA
- Hard negative mining from BM25 is critical for training effective dense retrievers
- In-batch negative training is efficient and effective for dual-encoder models

Title: Self-RAG — Learning to Retrieve, Generate, and Critique through Self-Reflection
Authors: Asai et al., 2023

Abstract: Despite the success of RAG, existing methods retrieve indiscriminately —
regardless of whether retrieval is needed — and never verify whether retrieved passages
actually support the generated output. Self-RAG trains a single LM to adaptively
retrieve passages on demand and generate special reflection tokens to self-evaluate its
own output. The model learns four types of reflection token: Retrieve (should I
retrieve?), IsRel (is this passage relevant?), IsSup (does the passage support my
claim?), and IsUse (is this response useful?). Self-RAG significantly outperforms
vanilla RAG and ChatGPT on six tasks spanning reasoning, fact verification, and
long-form generation — without sacrificing the versatility of the base LM.

Key findings:
- Adaptive retrieval prevents unnecessary retrieval and reduces latency
- Reflection tokens enable the model to self-correct without external critics
- Training with critic-guided data outperforms simple distillation
